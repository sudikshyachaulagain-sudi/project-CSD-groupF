Culture & Tradition

Traditional Dress

Nepali culture includes festivals like Dashain and Tihar, traditional dress, and diverse ethnic customs that reflect the country’s rich heritage. Dashain, the biggest festival, symbolizes the victory of good over evil and is celebrated with family gatherings, blessings, and rituals. Tihar, also known as the festival of lights, honors animals like crows, dogs, and cows, and is marked by colorful decorations and oil lamps. Traditional clothing such as the 'Daura Suruwal' for men and 'Sari' or 'Gunyo Cholo' for women represents cultural identity and is often worn during festivals and special occasions. Nepal is home to many ethnic groups like the Gurung, Sherpa, Newar, and Tharu, each with their own languages, traditions, dances, and foods. These diverse customs and traditions create a vibrant cultural environment where people celebrate unity in diversity while preserving their unique cultural practices.

  • Festivals: Dashain, Tihar
  • Dress: Daura Suruwal, Sari
  • Religion: Hinduism, Buddhism
