Nepali Festivals

Nepali festivals are vibrant celebrations that showcase the country's rich cultural heritage. From the grandeur of Dashain to the joy of Tihar, each festival brings communities together in tradition and festivity. These celebrations are marked by unique rituals, delicious foods, and colorful attire that reflect Nepal's diverse identity.

Dashain

Largest Nepali festival

Family gatherings, blessings, and celebratory rituals for good over evil.

Dashain is the most important festival in Nepal, celebrated over 15 days with worship of Goddess Durga, blessings from elders, and festive meals like sel roti and sweets.

Tihar

Festival of lights

Homes glow with lamps, music fills the streets, and prosperity is celebrated.

Tihar is a five-day festival honoring animals, wealth, and relationships. Families decorate their homes, worship Laxmi, and enjoy singing, dancing, and special foods.

Holi

Festival of colors

Spring arrives with vibrant powders, music, and joyful gatherings.

Holi celebrates the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. People throw colors, dance, sing, and savor sweets as families and communities come together.

Maghi

Tharu community festival

Traditional foods, cultural dances, and community celebration.

Maghi marks the Nepali New Year for the Tharu community, celebrated with special dishes, prayers, dances, and family gatherings for happiness and prosperity.
